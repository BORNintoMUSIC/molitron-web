## Voice

- Lead with the owner's problem and the next useful decision.
- Explain technical terms the first time they appear.
- Be direct about product fit and limitations.
- Prefer `may`, `can`, `up to`, and project-specific qualification where performance varies.
- Never turn listing, performance, or installation history into an approval guarantee.
- Avoid inflated phrases such as `revolutionary`, `best-in-class`, or `eliminates every odor`.

## Visual direction

The approved direction is **precision industrial**:

- Light-first interface; no public dark-mode toggle.
- Deep mineral teal, warm off-white, graphite, and stainless-steel neutrals.
- Restrained safety amber for warnings, status, and legacy cues.
- Modern engineered sans-serif typography.
- Authentic equipment photography before atmospheric imagery.
- Subtle data-plate and technical-drawing references, not generic SaaS effects.
- Preserve real product geometry and installation conditions in every image treatment.

## Logos and installation references

Confirmed customer names and logos may be used as installation references. Source logos from official brand resources, optimize locally, and do not imply sponsorship, partnership, or endorsement.
